Web Appendix A. SCENARIO IN STUDY1

You will be randomly assigned to a work scenario. Please read carefully and gradually imagine that you are in the situation described. Try to place yourself in the position of the protagonist and visualize the scenario as vividly as possible.

 

Condition 1: Presence of Algorithmic Management*High Inclusive Leadership

Imagine that you are Wang, a marketing employee at SPARK, an international hotel chain. You are in charge of channel development, online marketing, and offline marketing. Recently, the company has introduced an automatic algorithmic rating system for personnel management, and your daily work is monitored by this system. The system incorporates daily time tracking to monitor your attendance punctuality and working hours, as well as an automatic task allocation module that assigns tasks based on real-time workload data and employee-skill compatibility. Your workload and efficiency are analyzed by algorithms, which calculate the volume of tasks you process and the time required for each task to identify areas where you need to improve efficiency. Performance evaluation is highly detailed: natural language processing is applied to analyze customer service interactions such as call recordings and email communications. This analysis assesses service quality by quantifying response time, problem-solving efficiency, and customer satisfaction to support performance appraisal. A substantial portion of your compensation is determined by the automatic algorithmic rating system.

You work in the marketing department and are supervised by Manager Li. Manager Li is willing to listen to your new ideas and solutions at work, actively seeks opportunities to optimize work processes, and discusses the organizational vision and its implementation with you. You can consult him whenever you encounter work difficulties, as he is consistently present in the team and readily accessible. You can communicate with him in a timely manner when seeking professional advice. In general, Manager Li patiently listens to your thoughts and suggestions, encourages you to discuss problems identified in daily work, and constantly collaborates with you to explore new solutions to emerging issues.

 

Condition 2: Presence of Algorithmic Management*Low Inclusive Leadership

Imagine that you are Wang, a marketing employee at SPARK, an international hotel chain. You are in charge of channel development, online marketing, and offline marketing. Recently, the company has introduced an automatic algorithmic rating system for personnel management, and your daily work is monitored by this system. The system incorporates daily time tracking to monitor your attendance punctuality and working hours, as well as an automatic task allocation module that assigns tasks based on real-time workload data and employee-skill compatibility. Your workload and efficiency are analyzed by algorithms, which calculate the volume of tasks you process and the time required for each task to identify areas where you need to improve efficiency. Performance evaluation is highly detailed: natural language processing is applied to analyze customer service interactions such as call recordings and email communications. This analysis assesses service quality by quantifying response time, problem-solving efficiency, and customer satisfaction to support performance appraisal. A substantial portion of your compensation is determined by the automatic algorithmic rating system.

You work in the marketing department and are supervised by Manager Li. Manager Li is unwilling to listen to your new ideas or solutions in daily work and ignores opportunities to improve work processes. He generally refuses to discuss the organizational vision and its implementation with you. You cannot always consult him when facing work problems, as he is often absent from the team. He is unavailable when you attempt to seek professional guidance. In general, Manager Li declines to listen patiently to your thoughts and suggestions, does not encourage you to discuss work-related problems, and is unreachable for collaborative discussions on new solutions and challenges.

 

Condition 3: Absence of Algorithmic Management*High Inclusive Leadership

Imagine that you are Wang, a marketing employee at SPARK, an international hotel chain. You are in charge of channel development, online marketing, and offline marketing. In your daily work, the company highly respects employee privacy. It does not monitor your work computer (including work emails, website browsing history, and data transmission) or wiretap your work calls. The company stipulates that your daily work is directly supervised by your superior, who assigns tasks based on your skill compatibility and workload. Performance appraisal, which largely determines your compensation, is conducted primarily by your direct supervisor.

You work in the marketing department and are supervised by Manager Li. Manager Li is willing to listen to your new ideas and solutions at work, actively seeks opportunities to optimize work processes, and discusses the organizational vision and its implementation with you. You can consult him whenever you encounter work difficulties, as he is consistently present in the team and readily accessible. You can communicate with him in a timely manner when seeking professional advice. In general, Manager Li patiently listens to your thoughts and suggestions, encourages you to discuss problems identified in daily work, and constantly collaborates with you to explore new solutions to emerging issues.

Web Appendix B. SCALES IN STUDY1

Algorithmic Management(1 = Strongly Disagree, 7 = Strongly Agree; Parent-Rocheleau et al. 2024)

1. An algorithmic management system closely tracks me to ensure that I am completing my tasks.

2. An algorithmic management system closely monitors me while I am working.

3. Algorithmic management conducts a careful check of my work.

4. I am constantly monitored by an algorithmic management system to ensure my compliance with work-related rules.

5. My daily tasks are assigned by an algorithmic management system.

6. An algorithmic management system determines the tasks I am to perform.

7. In my work, an algorithmic management system defines what needs to be done.

8. An algorithmic management system sets the goals I must achieve at work (productivity goals, time goals, sales goals, etc.).

9. The goals I have to meet are set by an algorithmic management system.

10. An algorithmic management system decides when I work and when I do not.

11. My work schedule is formulated by an algorithmic management system.

12. An algorithmic system is responsible for determining my working hours.

13. My working hours are automatically determined by an electronic system.

14. My job performance appraisal is processed by an electronic system.

15. An algorithmic management system generates metrics used to evaluate my performance.

16. My performance appraisal is based on metrics calculated by an algorithmic management system.

17. A large portion of my compensation is determined by an algorithmic management system.

18. Decisions related to my income are mainly made by an algorithmic management system.

19. An algorithmic management system is responsible for calculating my salary without human intervention.

20. The compensation I receive is solely the result of calculations by an algorithmic management system.

 

Inclusive Leadership(1 = Strongly Disagree, 7 = Strongly Agree; Carmeli, Reiter-Palmon, and Ziv 2010)

1. My leader is open to hearing my new ideas.

2. My leader pays attention to new opportunities for improving work processes.

3. My leader is willing to discuss desired goals and new methods to achieve them.

4. My leader can provide advice on my problems.

5. I can easily reach my team leader.

6. My leader can discuss professional issues with me.

7. My leader is glad to hear our requests.

8. My leader encourages me to contact him/her when encountering unexpected problems.

9. My leader has enough time to discuss emerging issues with me when necessary.

 

Self-objectification(1 = Strongly Disagree, 7 = Strongly Agree; Wang et al. 2022)

Self-objectification--self-instrumentality:

1. In my work, being useful to the company or algorithms is more important than feeling satisfied within myself.

2. How I help the algorithms in the company to function properly is more important than my own thoughts or feelings.

3. Whether or not I am useful to the company makes me valuable to the company.

4. It is easy for someone with the same qualities and skills as me to replace me.

5. The best way to secure a position within a group is by being valuable to the company or algorithm.

6. One must be useful to the algorithm and the company to maintain the status of the work.

7. I need to be clear about whether I am useful to the company or algorithm.

8. I should understand to make the best use of my characteristics, such as gender, appearance and organizational status, to achieve my goals.

Self-objectification--self-denial of humanness:

I feel_________

1. that I am open-minded and capable of thinking clearly at work (reverse-coded).

2. responsive, enthusiastic, and very emotional (reverse-coded).

3. shallow and without depth of thought.

4. coldness like machines and emotionless like robots.

5. that I am refined and qualified (reverse-coded).

6. that I am intelligent, rational, and logical in my work (reverse-coded).

7. that I lack self-discipline at work and am undisciplined like an animal.

8. that I am immature in my behavior and ideas at work.

 

Exploratory Learning(1 = Strongly Disagree, 7 = Strongly Agree; Liu et al. 2024)

1. I strive to acquire new knowledge to develop hotel products or marketing plans that can lead me into entirely new learning areas (e.g., AI content generation, metaverse marketing).

2. I collect novel market information and creative ideas that go beyond my current hotel marketing work and experience.

3.  I plan to gather new information that can drive me to learn new skills and trends in the field of smart hotel services.

 

Exploitative Learning(1 = Strongly Disagree, 7 = Strongly Agree; Liu et al. 2024)

1. When faced with new service or marketing problems, I prioritize finding universal and proven methods and solutions within the hotel industry.

2. I apply information acquisition methods to help us understand and optimize the existing customer experience and marketing activities.

3. I focus on utilizing automation tools and algorithm knowledge related to my existing hotel marketing experience (e.g., existing customer relationship management systems, booking engine algorithms).

Exploratory Learning (1 = Strongly Disagree, 7 = Strongly Agree; Kostopoulos and Bozionelos 2011):

1. I have developed new capabilities for myself.

2. I have started to undertake new tasks that I was not familiar with before.

3. I have acquired new knowledge.

4. I have expanded my knowledge in entirely new fields for me.

5. I have learned to perform completely new tasks.

 

Exploitative Learning (1 = Strongly Disagree, 7 = Strongly Agree; Kostopoulos and Bozionelos 2011)：

1. Make good use of my existing capabilities.

2. Do a good job in my daily work.

3. Use the knowledge I currently have at work.

4. Consolidate my skills and knowledge in areas that support my existing work capabilities.

5. Learn to better complete existing work tasks.

 

Hotel Employees' Creativity (1 = Strongly Disagree, 7 = Strongly Agree; Wang and Netemeyer  2004):

1. I interact with customers in a creative way.

2. I can provide services to customers in a flexible manner.

3. I can put forward novel and practical ideas to meet customer needs.

4. For new customer problems, I can propose multiple solutions and evaluate them.

5. I put forward novel and practical suggestions for improving customer service.

6. I can improvise solutions to problems when there are no ready-made ones available.

7. I can propose new and effective methods to complete work.
